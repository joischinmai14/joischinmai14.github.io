# portfolio
# Chinmai Jois

*Designing between waste, memory, and movement.*

This is not just a portfolio.
It is a collection of fragments—
of materials that once existed elsewhere,
of stories carried through craft,
of garments shaped by purpose before trend.

---

## The Practice

I work at the intersection of
**sustainability · performance · cultural memory**

Taking what is discarded, overlooked, or industrial—
and reimagining it into forms that move, breathe, and endure.

Every piece begins with a constraint:
waste, function, terrain, climate.
And within that constraint, design finds its voice.

---

## The Language

* Upcycled textiles and post-industrial materials
* Modular, transformable silhouettes
* Outdoor performance as a design lens
* Indian craft narratives reinterpreted
* Garments that respond—to body, to movement, to environment

---

## Selected Works

**Kumbh Collection**
Ritual translated into form.
Movement, color, and devotion restructured into contemporary silhouettes.

**Kaudi Project**
Post-consumer waste meets Chittara—
hand-painted memory stitched onto reclaimed fabric.

**Modular Jackets**
Garments that shift identities—
reversible, androgynous, adaptable.
Designed not to be fixed, but to evolve.

---

## Current Chapter

Designing technical apparel at *Adventure Worx*—
where function is tested against terrain,
and garments are built for real conditions, not just concepts.

---

## Philosophy

I do not design to add more.
I design to rethink what already exists.

To reduce, to adapt, to question—
and to create garments that carry both intention and impact.

## Open
